04 | Methodology

Investment Logic

Two variables. Win rate and payoff — every trade in this portfolio is a composite decision made after weighing the two. Only two numbers decide the outcome: the probability this trade makes money (win rate), and how much you make when right versus lose when wrong (payoff). P/E is not the answer, ROE is not the answer, moats are not the answer — they are telescopes, not the thing seen through the telescope. When expected value is large enough, bet; when it is not, no story is good enough.

Anti-fragile multi-asset architecture. Yi Capital US is a USD multi-asset portfolio: equities provide offense; duration (US Treasuries) is the ammunition reserve for equity crashes — a 4–5% Treasury is essentially a put option struck at the price of the next panic; gold and rare metals hedge the tail risk of the fiat system; short-term bills and cash keep liquidity. The portfolio must not be destroyed by any single macro scenario — tariff war, AI-bubble burst, rate spike, credit crisis. The goal of anti-fragility is not to profit in a crisis, but to still be at the table after it.

Derivatives are psychological engineering, not speculation. Limited-cost bear spreads cover the foreseeable tail scenarios — not because a crash is predicted, but because its absence cannot be. The options execute the hedge before the psychological threshold is hit, transferring panic-zone decisions from a future self who may break down to a present self who is calm. What this protects is the floor of the payoff: in the worst case the loss is bounded, controlled, and never fatal.

Valuation anchoring, cycle rotation. No calling tops or bottoms. One thing only: consult the ten-year valuation distribution and build positions at statistical extremes — panic prices naturally raise the win rate (mean reversion is the most reproducible statistical regularity), and extreme discounts naturally amplify the payoff. Exit when valuation reverts to its fair range, however great the company.

Fat-tail risk control; reject the VaR illusion. Normal-assumption VaR precisely measures the part that needs no worrying, while staying silent on the tail that can actually destroy a portfolio. All risk on this page uses Cornish-Fisher / empirical quantiles plus Monte Carlo stress tests — measuring not "how much is lost on 99% of days," but "whether the loss stays bounded in the 1% that is truly lethal."

Staying at the table is the precondition for every positive-expectancy trade to pay off.
