Investment Highlights

Weixin and WeChat combined MAU stands at 1.432 billion, covering over ninety percent of Chinese internet users. FY2025 revenue reached RMB 751.8bn (+14%) with a 56% gross margin; Non-IFRS net profit attributable was RMB 259.6bn (+17%), operating cash flow RMB 347.8bn, year-end net cash RMB 107.1bn — alongside an equity investment portfolio worth roughly a trillion RMB. The company combines three natures at once: consumer monopoly, cash cow and investment holding.

The stock retraced more than forty percent from its October 2025 high of HK$683, touched HK$411 in June, then rebounded to about HK$470. The decline traces to doubled AI capex, reduced buybacks and a shrinking mark on the investment portfolio. Fundamentals moved the other way over the same period: 1Q26 operating profit excluding new AI products grew +17% YoY with margin rising to 43%, free cash flow +20%, net cash +63% — the gap between price and value is this report's starting point.

Anchoring at 22× FY2026E Non-IFRS EPS yields a target price of HK$770; a "core business + net cash + discounted investment portfolio" SOTP liquidation approach yields HK$615 as the margin-of-safety floor. The expanding evergreen game matrix, the release of Video Accounts ad load, and the payments take-rate option are the three things the market is not currently pricing. Initiating with BUY.

Chapter 1 — Company Overview (Excerpts)

1.2 Business Model: A Vertical Assembly of the World's Best Business Models

If one sentence must suffice: Tencent is not a company that owns several businesses, but an assembly that takes one copy of each of the world's proven best business models and vertically integrates them through Weixin. Payments is a duopoly toll booth; gaming is the deepest profit pool in global content; advertising is the steepest operating-leverage business in platform economics; cloud and enterprise services benchmark the business model that sustains the world's most valuable companies. The multiplier effect of vertical integration shows up in the financials as a 56.2% gross margin and a 1.35 cash-to-earnings ratio.

1.3 The Moat: Deeper Than the Sea, and Every Layer Charges a Toll

Ranked by replicability from hardest to easiest, the barriers stack in four layers: the social relationship graph (nearly impossible to replicate), ecosystem switching costs (extremely high), evergreen IP and the content portfolio (high), and capital plus distribution networks (medium-high). Attention can be stolen; the relationship chain cannot — this is the moat most frequently tested, at the highest cost of failure, in the history of the global internet.
